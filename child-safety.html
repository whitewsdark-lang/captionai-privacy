Child Safety Standards

Last updated: July 8, 2026

CaptionAI is committed to maintaining a safe environment and complying with all applicable child safety laws and regulations. This page describes our standards and practices regarding child safety.

1. App Purpose and Audience

CaptionAI is an AI-powered social media caption generator designed for content creators, businesses, and individuals aged 13 and above. The app is not directed at children under the age of 13.

2. No Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

CaptionAI has a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse material (CSAM) or any content that exploits or harms minors. We strictly prohibit:

  • Any content that sexually exploits minors
  • Any imagery, text, or material that depicts child abuse
  • Any use of our platform to facilitate harm to children

3. No User-Generated Content or Social Features

CaptionAI does not include any social networking features. Users cannot:

  • Share content with other users within the app
  • Communicate or interact with other users
  • Post or publish content publicly within the app
  • Upload images or files

The app only processes text input provided by the user to generate AI-powered captions. No content is stored or shared.

4. Reporting Mechanisms

If you encounter any content or behavior that may violate child safety standards, or if you have concerns related to child safety in connection with our app, please contact us immediately:

Email: ditdit37@gmail.com

We are committed to responding promptly to all child safety concerns and cooperating fully with relevant authorities.

5. Compliance with Laws

CaptionAI complies with all applicable child safety laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

  • The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)
  • The EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) provisions for minors
  • Google Play's Child Safety Standards Policy

We report any identified CSAM or child exploitation content to the appropriate national and regional authorities, including the National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) where applicable.

6. Contact for Child Safety Concerns

Our designated contact person for child safety matters is available to provide information about our CSAM prevention practices and policy compliance.
